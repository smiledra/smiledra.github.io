About me

I am interested in how people perceive and learn about the world. Within this frame, I have been studying speech as a particular medium, and examining how people learn, use, and represent speech in their brain. Currently, I am a PhD student at the University of Maryland, with affiliations in NACS (Program of Neuroscience and Cognitive Science), the Department of Linguistics, and the Department of Computer Science (which I am working on a Master's Degree in). For more of my research interest and specific projects, go to the research tab.

I am also a piano-player who plays what I love like a broken record. Other than performing, I also enjoy studying theories of music and Music History, occasionally composing parodies of the old time. I take music not as a career but very seriously, because as Seneca says:

Mihi crede, verum gaudium res severa est. (Sen. Ep. 23.4)

Chronologically, I am from Kunming, a city in Southwest China. Intriguingly, the official dialect of Kunming is a Northern dialect, with close relation to standard Mandarin. Here is a little project on my hometown dialect. I graduated from the University of Rochester in 2020, with a degree in Brain & Cognitive Sciences (B.S.) and another in Linguistics (B.A.).
